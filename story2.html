Reported Rapes in Queens go up 50 percent

Photo By: Denise Cervantes

How has the #MeToo Movement affected reported rape statistics in the city?

By Denise Cervantes

Nearly a year after the #MeToo movement, reports of rape are rising by about 20 percent across the city. In North Queens alone, reported rapes are up 50 percent, according to NYPD data, from 111 last year through October to 173 this year. In Elmhurst, rape reports doubled to 40, up from 21 last year.

The #MeToo movement erupted last October after The New York Times ran a story outlining the sexual harassment complaints against Hollywood producer’s Harvey Weinstein, and the link between the widely used social media hashtag and the increase in reported rapes is hard to dismiss.

Even though the increase in reported rapes seems to coincide with the case against Weinstein, law enforcement officials can’t prove the connection. But in a briefing earlier this year, Chief of Crime Control Strategies Dermot F. Shea pointed out that many of the reported crimes seem to be ones that occurred a long time ago.

“What we’re seeing is out of period, specifically, significantly older rapes are being reported,” said Shea.

Lindsey Pratt, psycotherapist, said since the #MeToo movement she has had more clients reach out wanting to specifically work through their sexual assault trauma.

"Only one in a hundred rape trials actually result in a conviction." - Lindsey Pratt

“The legal process for survivors can definitely come into play in therapy,” Pratt said. “There is a lot of difficulty in making those choices on whether or not to go forward and pressing charges.”
